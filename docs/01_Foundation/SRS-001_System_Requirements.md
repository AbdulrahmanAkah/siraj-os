# SRS-001 — Siraj System Requirements Specification

Version: 0.1 (Draft)

Status: Draft

Owner: SirajOS Architecture

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines WHAT the Siraj ecosystem must be capable of doing.

It intentionally avoids implementation details.

It defines capabilities, not technologies.

---

# 2. Scope

Siraj is an AI-assisted Knowledge Operating System specialized in producing trustworthy knowledge products.

The first application built on Siraj is the Islamic historical series "Siraj".

Future applications may include books, documentaries, educational courses, interactive experiences and other knowledge products.

---

# 3. Primary Objective

The system shall transform verified knowledge into publishable products while preserving historical accuracy, scholarly transparency and Islamic ethics.

---

# 4. Core Functional Requirements

## FR-001

The system shall maintain a structured knowledge model instead of isolated documents.

---

## FR-002

Every claim shall be traceable to one or more sources.

---

## FR-003

The system shall distinguish between:

- Facts
- Opinions
- Historical reports
- Scholarly interpretations
- Weak narrations
- Fabrications

---

## FR-004

The system shall preserve disagreements between scholars instead of hiding them.

---

## FR-005

The system shall support multiple output formats from the same knowledge base.

Examples:

- Documentary
- Book
- Article
- Video
- Interactive Timeline
- Educational Course

---

## FR-006

The system shall separate knowledge from presentation.

Changing the visual style must never change the knowledge.

---

## FR-007

Every generated asset shall contain provenance information.

The origin of every statement must remain recoverable.

---

## FR-008

The system shall support human review before publication.

AI never represents final authority.

---

## FR-009

The system shall allow future expansion without redesigning previous knowledge.

---

## FR-010

Knowledge shall exist independently from AI models.

Replacing an AI provider must not require rebuilding the knowledge base.

---

# 5. Non-Functional Requirements

Accuracy before speed.

Transparency before persuasion.

Documentation before automation.

Reusability before convenience.

Scalability before optimization.

Human supervision before autonomous publication.
